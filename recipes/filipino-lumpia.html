Filipino Lumpia

Description

Filipino lumpia is a crisp, savory spring roll enjoyed as a snack or appetizer at gatherings. Filled with a medley of vegetables and often ground pork, it delights with its crunchy exterior and flavorful interior.

Ingredients

Lumpia

  • 1/2 pound of ground pork
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup green beans, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • Lumpia wrappers (spring roll wrappers)
  • Cooking oil for frying

Sweet Chili Dipping Sauce

  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (for thickening)

Steps

Directions for Lumpia

  1. Prepare the Filling:
    • In a skillet over medium heat, cook ground pork until browned. Add the onions and garlic, and saut√© until fragrant.
    • Stir in carrots, green beans, cabbage, and green onions. Cook until vegetables are tender-crisp.
    • Season with soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat and let cool.
  2. Wrap the Lumpia:
    • Place a lumpia wrapper on a clean surface. Spoon filling onto the wrapper and shape into a log.
    • Fold the wrapper's sides over the filling, then roll tightly. Seal the edge with water.
  3. Fry the Lumpia:
    • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the lumpia until they are golden brown and crispy, turning them occasionally to ensure even cooking.
    • Remove from the oil and place on paper towels to absorb any excess oil.
  4. Serve and Enjoy:
    • Serve hot with a dipping sauce like sweet chili sauce or vinegar with garlic and chili. Enjoy your crispy Filipino lumpia!

Directions for Sweet Chili Dipping Sauce

  1. Combine Ingredients:
    • In a saucepan, combine rice vinegar, sugar, water, soy sauce, garlic, and red pepper flakes. Stir well.
  2. Cook the Sauce:
    • Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring frequently until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Thicken the Sauce:
    • Reduce heat to low. Stir in the cornstarch mixture. Continue to cook and stir until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  4. Cool and Serve:
    • Remove from heat and let cool. Serve as a dipping sauce for lumpia or other appetizers. Store any leftovers in the refrigerator.
